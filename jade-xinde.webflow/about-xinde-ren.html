Xinde Ren (b.2002) is a multidisciplinary artist based in London. She is currently pursuing the BSc Digital Art Computing at Goldsmiths, University of London. 
‍
Nowadays, humankind collectively enters an Odyssey from reality to virtuality. As more than half of the world's population becomes internet users, digital presence is integrated into human perception, memory and experience. We are in a liminal state, departing from the complete reality and sailing towards the complete virtuality. In digital liminality, uncertainty about the future has bred confusion and anxiety. The contradiction between the sense of alienation in interpersonal relationships and longing for intimacy is also intensified in this state. 
Technological progress is accelerating the restructuring of human relationships, directly or indirectly affecting real lives. Addressing the anxiety generated therein, satisfying individuals' desire to connect with the community, and breaking free from information cocoons demands a refreshing perspective urgently. 
‍
In the vast sphere of reality-virtuality continuum, my passion lies in the transformations that occur in interpersonal relationships. Combining emerging technologies and artistic expression, across digital creations, multi-sensory installations, paintings, and narratives, I try to soothe the anxieties that arise within the digital liminal spaces. My works are invitations to forge intimacy amid the seemingly boundless expanse of technology, redefining it as a unifying bond that transcends the ephemeral.
‍
In the midst of an age characterized by ceaseless digitalization and the unrelenting deluge of information, my works emerge as a profound meditation. My practice beckons people to mend the bonds between individuals and communities, utilizing the very virtual technologies that have been perceived as agents of division. My creative oeuvre stands as a testament to my belief in technology’s potential to mend, to unite, and to infuse lives with a renewed sense of shared humanity.
